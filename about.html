About

This is a self-assessment webapp. It's designed to help the user run experiments about their daily lives. The experiments run by this webapp don't need any fancy machinery or special knowledge. To run a successful experiment a user only has to follow instructions and a schedule.

You may wonder what kind of experiments we can run just using a website. When most people think of experiments they think of research labs, or hospitals, or social surveys that sample people from across the country. Those experiments are big and complicated because the scientists who run them are aiming for general truth -- they want to prove something about everyone everywhere (or close to it :) ). Our website will be focused on a much smaller scale : you! With this website and a little work, you'll be able to find out all sorts of things about yourself, and it won't be very difficult.

Self-assessment Webapp . . . Version 0.1.0

There are a lot of interesting questions you can answer with a webapp like this. Some good self-assessment question examples :

  1. Does taking Melatonin help me sleep better?
  2. Does the weather have a big impact on my mood?
  3. Does listening to music while I work hurt my productivity?
  4. Does eating breakfast in the morning improve my ability to focus?

It's not hard to come up with these: you only need an object of your curiousity and a way to measure it. Those questions were some of my motivating examples when building this webapp. Unfortunately, the webapp is far from complete : we'll be below version 1 until I finish learning JavaScript. For now, the only question this webapp can help you answer is number 4. Continue reading to see how it works!

Getting Started

The first thing you'll need to do is create an account, but this is done automatically when you create a new assessment schedule. You first stop, then, should be the New Schedule link. Once you've created a schedule, be sure to stop back by the Welcome page. You'll get your daily intervention instructions there. Afterwards, head to the SART (Sustained Attention to Response Task) Assessment page and read through the tutorial. Don't worry -- the site will explain what SART is once you're there. After you do the tutorial, you're done for now. Just regularly check back in at the appropriate times, follow the instructions, and complete the SART assessmenets. Your results will be viewable once your schedule is over.
